# First Notice of Loss (FNOL) Submission Guide

Welcome to the AI-Automated Auto Insurance Claims Platform. This guide is for policyholders (claimants) submitting a new claim.

## What is an FNOL?

FNOL stands for First Notice of Loss. It is the initial report made to an insurance provider following loss, theft, or damage of an insured asset (in this case, your vehicle).

Our platform simplifies this process, allowing you to submit a claim and receive an initial assessment in under 5 minutes.

## The 3-Step Process

The FNOL intake form is a simple, multi-step process designed to gather all necessary information quickly and securely.

### Step 1: Claimant & Policy Information

1. Navigate to the FNOL Submission Portal (e.g., `https://claims.your-insurance-platform.com`).
2. Click **Start a Claim**.
3. Provide your basic contact information:
   - Full Name
   - Phone Number
   - Email Address
4. Provide your **Policy Number**. This is essential for verifying your coverage.
5. Click **Next Step**.

### Step 2: Incident Details

In this step, you will describe the accident or incident.
1. **Incident Date:** Select the date the damage occurred.
2. **Incident Location:** Provide the address or a detailed description of where the incident happened (e.g., "Main St. and 1st Ave intersection, Springfield").
3. **Incident Description:** Briefly explain what happened (e.g., "Rear-ended at a red light by another vehicle").
4. Click **Next Step**.

### Step 3: Vehicle Information & Damage Photos

This is the most critical step for our AI to assess your claim accurately.
1. **Vehicle Details:** Provide your vehicle's VIN, Make, Model, and Year.
2. **Upload Photos:** You must provide clear photos of the damage.
   - **Quality:** Ensure photos are well-lit and clearly show the affected areas.
   - **Angles:** Try to include wide shots showing the entire vehicle and close-up shots of the specific damage.
   - **Format:** We accept JPEG and PNG files (Max 5MB per photo, up to 10 photos total).
   - Use the drag-and-drop area or click to select files from your device.
3. Click **Submit Claim**.

## What Happens Next?

Upon submission:
1. You will receive an immediate confirmation email with your unique **Claim Number** (e.g., `CLM-2025-001234`).
2. The platform's AI will begin analyzing your photos and incident details.
3. If your claim qualifies for auto-approval (minor damage, clear photos, active policy), you may receive an estimated repair cost and approval notification within minutes.
4. If your claim requires human review (complex damage, blurry photos, etc.), an adjuster will review it promptly. You will be notified of their decision via email.
5. You can use the **Claimant Portal** to check the status of your claim at any time using your Claim Number.
